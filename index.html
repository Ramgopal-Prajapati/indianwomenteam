About Indian Women's Cricket

The journey from inception to global recognition

The Indian women's cricket team represents India in international women's cricket. Governed by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI), the team played its first Test match in 1976 against the West Indies.

Over the years, the team has grown in stature and performance, with notable achievements including reaching the finals of the 2005 World Cup, 2017 World Cup, and 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup.

The historic 2025 World Cup victory marked a new era for women's cricket in India, inspiring millions of young girls to take up the sport.

Today, the team boasts world-class players across all formats and continues to make the nation proud with their performances on the global stage.
